# Game Ideas — Week 18, 2026
> Based on market research from 2026-04-27

---

### Idea 1: Beat Blitz
**Genre:** Rhythm

**Core mechanic:** Players tap arrows or keys in sync with music as notes scroll across the screen — but compete in real-time against up to 4 players, with the winner determined by accuracy percentage at the end of the song.

**Monetisation model:** Cosmetic-only — sell custom note skin packs and song-select game passes for premium song libraries.

**Why now:** Funky Friday (the dominant Roblox rhythm game) is 4 years old with flat player growth. DevForum has 20+ posts requesting a "new rhythm game" or citing frustration with Funky Friday's limited song selection. No credible competitor has launched in 2 years.

**Estimated complexity:** Medium (6–10 development nights for v1)

**Suggested first milestone:** Single-player mode with 5 songs, note scrolling, and accuracy scoring. Multiplayer and cosmetics can follow in M2.

---

### Idea 2: Greenfield — Cozy Survival
**Genre:** Survival

**Core mechanic:** Players gather resources, craft tools, and build shelters in a procedurally-arranged world — but the game is fully PvE with no griefing. Players can collaborate with others or play solo. Seasons cycle every 2 real-world days, changing available resources and ambient weather.

**Monetisation model:** Game passes — sell a "Builder's Pack" with extra inventory slots and a "Season Pass" with cosmetic season-themed building materials.

**Why now:** No top-20 Roblox survival game exists. Multiple DevForum threads specifically request a survival game without PvP griefing. The Minecraft-adjacent survival niche on Roblox is almost entirely unserved by quality games.

**Estimated complexity:** Large (15+ nights for a compelling v1)

**Suggested first milestone:** Resource gathering, basic crafting, shelter building, and solo play. Day/night cycle. No seasons or multiplayer in M1.

---

### Idea 3: Neon Dash — Competitive Obby
**Genre:** Obby

**Core mechanic:** A short (90-second), extremely fast-paced obstacle course set in a neon/cyberpunk environment where players race against each other to reach the finish. The map changes every 10 minutes (rotating from a pool of 20 hand-crafted maps).

**Monetisation model:** Cosmetic-only — sell custom character trails, hat accessories, and map-specific win emotes.

**Why now:** Tower of Hell (the obby benchmark) is 5 years old with flat growth. The obby genre is structurally strong (short sessions, inherently competitive) but the #1 game is stale. A modern, visually striking obby with rotating maps could capture obby players looking for something fresh.

**Estimated complexity:** Small (4–6 nights for a compelling v1 with 5 maps and basic cosmetics)

**Suggested first milestone:** 5 maps, time-trial race for up to 8 players, finish-line leaderboard. Cosmetics and map rotation in M2.

---

### Idea 4: Dojo Masters — Fighting RPG (Accessible)
**Genre:** Fighting/RPG

**Core mechanic:** Players choose a fighting style (3 styles in v1: sword, staff, unarmed), level it up through PvP matches, and unlock new moves as they progress. The game is differentiated by an aggressive tutorial that teaches combos in an interactive practice arena before players enter PvP.

**Monetisation model:** Hybrid — cosmetic skins are free-tier, a "Style Unlock" game pass unlocks the third fighting style early (otherwise earned at level 15).

**Why now:** Type Soul and Wisteria (both trending up) prove demand for anime-inspired fighting RPGs, but player reviews consistently note steep learning curves and poor tutorials. An accessible entry in this genre with a strong tutorial could capture the audience the incumbents are losing due to frustration.

**Estimated complexity:** Medium (8–12 nights for v1 with 2 fighting styles and tutorial)

**Suggested first milestone:** 2 fighting styles, basic combo system, interactive tutorial arena, PvP match with win/loss XP. Leveling and third style in M2.

---

## Recommendation Ranking

1. **Neon Dash — Competitive Obby** — Highest priority this week because it has the smallest complexity, a clear visual identity gap in the market, and the obby genre has extremely broad appeal on Roblox. A polished v1 could be ready in one week of development nights. Low risk, fast feedback loop.

2. **Beat Blitz** — Second priority because the rhythm genre demand signal is very strong (documented DevForum requests, stale incumbent) and the core mechanic is well-understood. Medium complexity but the mechanics are entirely scripting — no novel asset pipeline challenges.

3. **Dojo Masters** — Consider for a future sprint after evaluating how Sword Arena (currently in development) performs in the fighting genre. Launching two fighting games simultaneously would divide the internal focus.

4. **Greenfield (Cozy Survival)** — Lowest priority this week despite the genuine market gap, because the Large complexity estimate means it would consume 15+ development nights before a v1 is shippable. Revisit when the team has additional capacity.
